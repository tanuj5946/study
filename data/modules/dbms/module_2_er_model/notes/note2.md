### Relationships and Constraints

A Relationship represents association between two or more entities.

Example:
Student "enrolls in" Course.

Relationship Set:
Collection of similar relationships.

Degree of Relationship:

1. Unary Relationship  
   Relationship between entities of same type.

2. Binary Relationship  
   Relationship between two different entity sets (most common).

3. Ternary Relationship  
   Relationship among three entity sets.

Cardinality Constraints

Cardinality defines number of entities participating in relationship.

Types:

1. One-to-One (1:1)  
   One entity associated with only one entity.

2. One-to-Many (1:N)  
   One entity associated with multiple entities.

3. Many-to-One (N:1)  
   Multiple entities associated with one entity.

4. Many-to-Many (M:N)  
   Multiple entities associated with multiple entities.

Participation Constraints

1. Total Participation  
   Every entity must participate in relationship.

2. Partial Participation  
   Participation is optional.

Exam Notes:
- Cardinality questions are common in exams.
- Draw diagram practice required.
